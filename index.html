Tips from your Airbnb host
Insider picks for your redwood days

Make a day of Trinidad heading north

Don't miss this awesome beach town right on the 101. Luffenholtz Beach (via the scenic route) is a local treasure and was recently featured on the cover of Sunset magazine — the Trinidad coast will take your breath away.

Start the morning at the Beach Comber Cafe — wonderful breakfast, but bring your own mug (they don't sell coffee in to-go cups, though they'll lend you one). Then walk it off on the Trinidad Head trail: easy 1.6-mile round trip. Park at Trinidad State Beach and find the trailhead there.

Fern Canyon — call ahead

Famous for the fern-covered canyon walls, a creek running through it, and a beach right alongside. Sure to please. Expect a semi-bumpy dirt road in and an $8 entrance fee. The park has a reservation system now — call (707) 465-7354 before you go.

Add Trillium Falls — a guest favorite

A lesser-known gem in the same vicinity as Fern Canyon. Short 2-mile round trip through giant redwoods, trillium blooms (in season), and a little waterfall at the end.

Prairie Creek: start at the ranger station

Take the exit for Newton B. Drury Scenic Byway / Prairie Creek State Park and park at the ranger station. Pick up a map and hit the trails behind it — the host's favorite loops are the James Irvine loop and the Miner's Ridge loop. Make them as short or long as you want; giant redwoods either way.

On Newton B. Drury Parkway, watch for a stop on the left marked "the Corkscrew Tree" — a short hike to one of the host's favorite redwoods. And don't skip Lady Bird Johnson Grove while you're in the area.

Howland Hill Road into Jedediah Smith

On your way to Crescent City, plug "Howland Hill" into GPS. At the road, take a right and follow it up the hill until you hit a dirt road — about 7 miles off the 101. Plenty of pullouts for photos and hikes. One of the host's favorite roads, and one of the best places to see giant redwoods.
