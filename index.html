1
San Francisco
2 nights · Tue Jun 2 – Wed Jun 3 · No car needed

Get the city out of the way while everyone's fresh. Stay near Fisherman's Wharf, use transit and rideshare. Pick up the rental car at end of day 2.

🏝
Alcatraz Island
Ferry to the famous prison island. The audio tour is incredible — even the boys will be hooked.
Book now — sells out months ahead
🌉
Golden Gate Bridge
Walk or bike across. Vista Point on the north side has the best photo ops of the city skyline.
Must do
🦭
Pier 39 & Sea Lions
Watch the sea lions lounging on the docks. Grab clam chowder in a bread bowl at Boudin Bakery.
Kids will love
🚋
Cable Car Ride
It's basically a roller coaster through the city streets. Ride the Powell-Hyde line for the best views.
Kids will love
🍫
Ghirardelli Square
Free chocolate samples and massive sundaes. End the evening here for a sweet finish.
Treat stop
🏮
Chinatown
The oldest Chinatown in North America. Walk through Dragon's Gate and explore the side alleys.
Worth a walk
3 hours
Hwy 101 north
Pick up rental car. Stop at Skunk Train in Willits (steam engine through redwoods — runs Wed/Fri/Sat/Sun, book ahead).
2
Fort Bragg & Mendocino
1 night · Thu Jun 4 · Coastal charm

A tiny village on the cliffs with no chain stores. Break up the drive north and enjoy a coastal sunset before the redwoods begin.

💎
Glass Beach
A beach covered in colorful sea glass from decades of dumping, now polished smooth by the waves. The boys will love treasure hunting.
Must do
🌊
Mendocino Headlands
Dramatic coastal bluffs with sea caves, blowholes, and easy cliff-top walking trails. Incredible at sunset.
Easy walk
🚂
Skunk Train, Willits
Historic steam engine ride through redwood canyons. A magical introduction to the big trees. Stop here on the drive from SF.
Book in advance
3 hours
Redwood Highway north
Drive-Thru Tree in Leggett, Confusion Hill, then the 31-mile Avenue of the Giants through Humboldt Redwoods. Swim in the Eel River.
3
Trinidad & the Redwoods
5 nights · Fri Jun 5 – Tue Jun 9 · Home base
Core of the trip

One Airbnb, five nights, and the tallest trees on Earth in every direction. Every day is a different adventure — all within 30–60 minutes of your base. This is why you came.

🌿
Fern Canyon
50-foot walls dripping with ferns. Filmed in Jurassic Park 2. You walk through a stream — bring waterproof shoes. Elk on nearby Gold Bluffs Beach.
Can't miss
🌲
Lady Bird Johnson Grove
Easy 1.5-mile loop through ancient old-growth. One of the most peaceful walks you'll ever take. Perfect for kids.
Easy · 1.5 mi
🚡
Trees of Mystery + SkyTrail
Giant Paul Bunyan statues, a gondola ride through the treetops, suspension bridges in the canopy. Boys will love every second.
Kids will love
🏔
Jedediah Smith Redwoods
The biggest trees in the park system. Drive Howland Hill Road through old-growth, hike to Stout Grove (340 ft tall, 22 ft diameter). Swim in the crystal-clear Smith River.
Must do
🚗
Prairie Creek Scenic Drive
Newton B. Drury Scenic Parkway — 10 miles through redwoods with elk herds, the Big Tree (1,500 years old), and pullouts everywhere.
Scenic drive
🛶
Trinity River Whitewater Rafting
Full-day Class II-III rapids with Six Rivers Rafting. Age 7+. Includes organic lunch, waterfall hike, and cliff jumping. 4.9 star rating.
Book in advance
🏖
Gold Bluffs Beach
Wild beach where Roosevelt elk graze on the sand. Just down the road from Fern Canyon. Feels like another world.
Unique
🌅
Trinidad Head Trail
Easy coastal loop right near your base with panoramic ocean views. Perfect sunset hike. Cute fishing village for dinner after.
Easy · sunset
🏠
Crescent City + Lighthouse
Battery Point Lighthouse is accessible only at low tide. Walk out to it, explore the museum, and enjoy the rugged northern coast.
Low tide only
3 hours
East & south inland
Leave the coast. Stunning drive through mountains to Mt. Shasta, then south to Burney Falls and Sundial Bridge.
4
Redding & Mt. Shasta
1 night · Wed Jun 10 · Transition stop

A day of volcanoes, waterfalls, and glass bridges as you loop inland and head south toward wine country. Big scenery with minimal effort.

🗻
Mount Shasta
14,179-foot snow-capped volcano visible for 100 miles. Grab lunch in the charming little mountain town at its base.
Iconic view
💧
Burney Falls
Called the "8th wonder of the world" by Teddy Roosevelt. 129-foot waterfall fed by underground springs. Easy 1-mile loop trail.
Must do
🌁
Sundial Bridge, Redding
Stunning glass-and-steel pedestrian bridge designed by Santiago Calatrava. Turtle Bay Exploration Park nearby if the boys want more.
Quick stop
3 hours
South through Sacramento Valley
Easy highway drive south through the valley to Sonoma wine country. Rolling green hills and vineyards.
5
Healdsburg & Russian River
3 nights · Thu Jun 11 – Sat Jun 13 · Wind down

The perfect decompression after a week in the woods. Float the river, eat ice cream in a charming town square, and visit one last redwood grove before heading home.

🛟
Russian River Lazy Tubing
Float from Steelhead Beach to Sunset Beach — about 4.5 hours of pure relaxation. Rent tubes on site. Warm water, mellow current.
Full day
🌲
Armstrong Redwoods
A small but stunning old-growth redwood grove right in wine country. Easy flat trails, perfect for families. One last dose of the giants.
Easy walk
🍦
Healdsburg Plaza
Charming town square with boutique shops, ice cream, wine tasting rooms for the adults, and a relaxed small-town vibe.
Relax
1.5 hours
South to San Francisco
Easy drive back to the city. Last evening in SF — grab a nice dinner to celebrate the trip.
6
San Francisco
1 night · Sun Jun 14 · Last night

Stay near SFO or back in the city for one final evening. Wake up slow Monday morning, grab breakfast, and head to the airport. No rush — your flight isn't until 11:37 AM.

Mon Jun 15
Fly home
Drop rental car at SFO. Flight departs 11:37 AM, arrive Orlando 11:17 PM. Trip of a lifetime complete.
